Southampton Left Slate

The people listed below are all supporters of Jeremy Corbyn and the leadership of our Party. They fully support our Leaders 10 pledges which include a fully funded public NHS, a mass social/council house building program, a national comprehensive education service, public ownership of railways and other services.

Lisa Fricker

Secretary

I have vast experience of many administrative roles dating back to the late 1980's including secretary of Coxford and Millbrook Labour Party wards.

I have successfully run my own childminding business for the last 7 years and have held many roles within PACEY which is an organisation representing Early Years practitioners, including Facilitator for Southampton and National Policy Forum Rep. I am currently a School governor of a Southampton Primary School.

I am also a member of Unite Community and was the founding member of Momentum in Southampton after helping in the first election of Jeremy Corbyn as Leader and bringing Jeremy to our City for a massive rally in 2015. I have helped Momentum Southampton to grow and brought new members to the Labour Party locally and encouraged others to rejoin and become active.

I believe that the success of any organisation lies partly with the actions of its secretary and with the skills and experience that I have gained over the years through my paid and voluntary work I know that I have the qualities that a good secretary should possess. I am methodical, well organised, reliable, honest and have good I.T. Skills to name but a few.

I fully support the leadership of our Party and its Socialist policies including a secure publicly-provided NHS, National Education Service, mass social/council house building, protecting our planet and public ownership of our railways.

I will work closely with the other members of the E.C. to make sure that Southampton and Romsey Labour Party is inclusive, democratic and continues to grow. I look forward to your support.

Beccy Ruddick

Assistant Secretary and IT Officer

I would like to stand for the role of assistant secretary, and believe I have many skills and attributes relevant to this role.

I am a Trade Union (USDAW) rep in a local supermarket, which places me at the heart of the lowest paid vulnerable working class community which the Labour Party was formed to empower.

Through my Trade Union activities I have developed skills in organising, I.T., administration, note-taking and various presentation and meeting skills.

I am currently on USDAW's prestigious Organising Academy, a model on which the TUC has based its own Academy. This has allowed me to take my organisational skills to the next level.

I would be absolutely delighted to take on a role in a Labour Party committed to the Socialist policies of our leadership, policies which will seek to eradicate many inequalities in our society. I would work closely with the Secretary and other EC officers giving them my full support.

Andy Frampton

Membership Officer

As your EC Membership Officer I will, alongside other members of Executive team work to;

  1. Retain existing members

    Recognising that some members are not as actively involved but that every contribution and support is vital for the lifeblood of the CLP and that the membership records and payments are up to date.

  2. Recruit new members

    Membership is vital as a building capacity and developing our local campaigning ability. Encourage us all to get into the habit of promoting membership.

  3. Provide a welcoming and supportive environment

    All members longstanding and new should be made welcome and included, regardless of their interest, experiences and skills. To encourage members involvement on issues that matter to them.

  4. Keep all members up to date and informed on Meetings, Campaigns, and Events

As your Membership Officer I would continue to learn and up date my ability for this role to ensure that this Labour Party continues to grow, remains vibrant, diverse and inclusive. Many thanks.

Alan Fraser

Political Education Officer

I have been a member of the Labour Party and lifelong trade union activist since 1970/80's. I have been active as a trade unionist first in the Building Trade and then in the Post Office for a number of years. I have held many positions in both the Labour Party and Trade unions such as Chair of a CLP, Branch Secretary of the CWU, President of a Trades Council as well as being a shop steward for many years. I have a practiced based history that has involved working with Black South African Trade Unions internationally. I believe fundamentally in equality/diversity and the political need to ensure our Party is reflective of this diversity and is inclusive to all sections of society.

I have been involved in political education since the 80's and worked with striking miners during the miners strike of 84/85 in this capacity. In the 80's I went on to study for a degree and a teaching qualification and started formally teaching shop stewards and fulltime trade union officials. I have also worked for the Open University as a tutor in the 90's teaching Social Science. I have a theoretical and practice based history in political education and have been teaching politics for over 30 years.

I am experienced, passionate and knowledgeable about political education and see this is as one of the most important issues/areas for our Party. I believe that in moving forward the Party must recognise the importance of political education and I believe with others I can play a role in helping to achieve this.

Gemma Griffiths

Women's Officer

My name is Gemma Griffiths, I live in the Southampton Test constituency. I would very much like to be your nominee for Womens' Officer. Here's a little bit about myself and why I am asking for your nomination.

I am a 37 year old teacher. I have been active within the NASUWT and have represented members in several workplaces as a NASUWT union representative. I have spent my life as a teacher teaching children and adults, including working extensively in the community with troubled families to support parents and their children to increase life chances and opportunities.

I strongly believe in the values of our party. I am inspired and highly motivated by Jeremy being elected again and the direction that our party is now moving in.

Our party has a great history of developing women and working towards equality. Ever since its formation our party has been the natural home for generations of women campaigning for social justice and equality. From the work of Margaret Bondfield, Ellen Wilkinson, Jenny Lee and Barbara Castle who paved the way towards where we are today, to women such as Diane Abbott and Angela Rayner who prove that women can be strong in key roles in the shadow cabinet, our party can be a great example to women and men alike. However, though these are good, solid foundations, we have a long way to go to achieve equality.

If I was to be elected to this position, I would like to work with our members and out in the community to engage women in a very practical way. This would develop skills, knowledge and confidence to enable women to play a full and active role. It would be my intention to work with others in the party collaboratively in order to build an inclusive party that represents all women and reflects diversity both within the party and our affiliates. Thank you for your time.
